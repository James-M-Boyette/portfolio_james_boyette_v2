Never Not Curious something something

One time, when I was six, I used my safety scissors to cut the cord of Mom's clothing iron. It was definitely plugged in.

I wish I could say I was attempting to give her a much-deserved 'day off.' Or that I was lodging some deep and worthy protest. Frankly, I was just fascinated by electrical current.

Result: my scissors never cut right again, and Mom says she saw me booking it past the kitchen doorway in my best "casual" speed-walk.

That is to say, I get a lot of energy from exploring and learning how something works. "How?" and "Why?" are some of my favorite questions.

As I understand something more deeply, I gain exponential efficiency. Knowing that "these things are in 'x' category" or that "this has a (un)common capability" shortcuts my deliberation, planning, and decision-making for whether and how to use things.

This clarity also drastically improves my communication. I am best at explaining my own needs, giving reasons, and other people's positions when I possess this kind of inter connected understanding.
